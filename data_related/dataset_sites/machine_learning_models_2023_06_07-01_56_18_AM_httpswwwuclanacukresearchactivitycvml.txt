Machine Learning (CVML)</h1></div></div></div><div><div class="ComposerItemstyled__ComposerItemStyled-sc-5b4dp1-0 kWsmKz"><div class="Containerstyled__ContainerStyled-sc-1fpx6q5-0 bKDMbo"><div class="row"><div class="narrowColumn"><p class="PageIntrostyled__PageIntroStyled-gvfn7n-0 jBTPQy">The Computer Vision and Machine Learning (CVML) Research Group emerged as a result of cross-disciplinary interests in research and applications related to Computer Vision, Machine Learning and Medical Image Computing. </p></div></div></div></div><div class="ComposerItemstyled__ComposerItemStyled-sc-5b4dp1-0 kfPrwM"><div class="Containerstyled__ContainerStyled-sc-1fpx6q5-0 bKDMbo"><div class="row"><div class="narrowColumn"><div class="ContentImagestyled__ContentImageStyled-czrgr9-0 glVzAw"><div class="figureBox"><img class="Imagestyled__ImageStyled-sc-1dsq35z-0 iLeOdn image" alt="cvml-research-banner.jpg" src="/image-library/imported-images/research/cvml-research-banner.xecabed45.jpg?width=740&amp;quality=75&amp;format=webp"/></div></div></div></div></div></div><div class="ComposerItemstyled__ComposerItemStyled-sc-5b4dp1-0 kWsmKz"><div class="Containerstyled__ContainerStyled-sc-1fpx6q5-0 bKDMbo"><div class="row"><div class="narrowColumn"><div class="Markupstyled__MarkupStyled-sc-15gc5dq-0 dvvHBE"><p>The main focus of the group is to research, develop and deploy novel methods for the analysis of visual information for medical and industrial applications. The group have led or been involved in a number of industry, EPSRC, FP7 and HEIF funded projects.</p>
<p>The ongoing emphasis is on developing new vision and machine learning algorithms and their transfer to real-world applications. The particular areas of interest include: Bayesian methodology for data modelling, pattern recognition and tracking; statistical shape analysis; deformation modelling for model-based recognition, segmentation and registration; medical imaging; intelligent energy management; data mining; and applications of deep learning.</p>
<p>The ongoing progress in the mathematical tools, the growing prevalence of very large datasets, ever-increasing computational power and progress in imaging devices established the computer vision and machine learning as mature scientific disciplines with a rapidly growing number of exploitations in wide spectrum of applications, such as machine condition monitoring, medical diagnosis, assistive living or autonomous vehicles to name just a few.</p></div><div class="MarkupRevealstyled__MarkupRevealStyled-sc-1i82376-0 jwsqCE"><button class="mrBtn">Read more<!-- --> <i class="IconFontstyled__IconStyled-sc-153oxd8-0 euRklp mrBtnIcon"></i></button><div class="Markupstyled__MarkupStyled-sc-15gc5dq-0 dvvHBE mrContent" aria-hidden="true"><p>The CVML group have a wide ranging skills, combing expertise from different disciplines, including: engineering, physics, computing and media. The group activities are supported by a dedicated research laboratory with various vision systems including a number of static and dynamic 3D scanning devices, multi-camera acquisition and lighting systems, as well as dedicated GPU computing facilities.</p>
<p>The activities of the group are further supported by the international collaborations, including Information Processing and System Teams (ETIS) at Cergy-Pontoise University, I3S laboratory at the University of Cote d&rsquo;Azur, or Signals and Images Laboratory at the Institute of the National Research Council of Italy (CNR) in Pisa, Computer Support for Medical Diagnosis &amp; Therapy Procedures &amp; Operations Group at the AGH University of Science and Technology in Krak&oacute;w.</p>
